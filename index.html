Sample outreach email

Sample Outreach Email: Testimonial Version

A ready-to-send email for inviting colleagues and community partners to the Gathering, built around reflections from past participants.

Subject
A virtual learning opportunity: 2026 Global Gathering for the Future of Child Welfare

Dear colleagues and community partners,

I am sharing an upcoming virtual learning opportunity that may be of interest to colleagues working across child welfare, community services, policy, leadership, advocacy, and prevention.

A Global Gathering for the Future of Child Welfare is an international three-day virtual convening that will take place October 6–8, 2026. Year to year, I have found it to be a valuable space for learning how leaders in other countries and jurisdictions are responding to the pressures and complexity of our work.

The event website describes the Gathering as intentionally designed to move beyond passive learning into active engagement, with Skill Building Institutes, interactive workshops, solution-oriented strategy sessions, creative spaces, and keynotes that invite participants to exchange ideas and explore new possibilities for child, youth, family, and community well-being. The spirit and purpose of the Gathering are encapsulated by this short video.

Past participants have described the Gathering as both practical and thought-provoking. A few participant reflections offer a sense of the depth, range, and quality of the experience:

  1. "I am not sure I can pick just one thing I love about this gathering. This was absolutely the most valuable and inspiring conference I have attended in terms of content, virtual or in-person, in my 25+ years in the field!"
    (2023; trainer/technical assistance provider, 11+ years; U.S.)
  2. "I really liked hearing what others were doing all over the world. I also liked that I could access the recorded sessions for a while. There were so many great presentations I was not able to attend that I could re-watch."
    (2025; prevention program manager, 16–20 years; Canada)
  3. "I valued listening to and learning from colleagues from different continents and organizations who were sharing similar ideas."
    (2024; trainer/technical assistance provider, 11+ years; Europe)
  4. "I valued learning about child welfare services across the globe and having opportunities to network."
    (2023; child welfare practitioner/kinship care provider, 6–10 years; Asia)
  5. "There were so many positive experiences. Parents from around the world uniting was very impactful, and hearing from courageous women who made their way through what must have felt impossible was powerful."
    (2024; community service agency administrator, 11+ years; Canada)

Group registration discounts are available for organizations sending multiple participants. This can make it easier for a team to attend together and carry the learning back into its local work. You can learn more and register here: www.futureofchildwelfare.org.

Please feel free to share this with colleagues or networks who may be interested.

Warmly,
[Your name]
[Your title, organization, or affiliation]

Event description copy

Ready-made descriptions of the Gathering at four lengths, for newsletters, social posts, intranet pages, or wherever you need copy that fits the space.

Three-paragraph version

A Global Gathering for the Future of Child Welfare is a bold, three-day virtual convening taking place October 6–8, 2026. It brings together people from around the world who are reimagining what child, youth, family, and community well-being can look like in the years ahead. Designed to move beyond traditional conference formats, the Gathering creates space for connection, creativity, lived experience, global perspectives, and future-focused thinking.

The event begins with immersive Skill Building Institutes—3.5-hour, hands-on learning experiences that give courageous learners time to practice new approaches, explore practical frameworks, and strengthen skills they can carry into their work. The following two days feature inspiring keynotes, interactive workshops, solution-oriented strategy sessions, international exchanges, and creative spaces that use dialogue, film, poetry, storytelling, and other forms of expression to open new possibilities.

Participants include those with lived experience, practitioners, supervisors, advocates, researchers, policymakers, community leaders, and emerging professionals. Across roles, disciplines, countries, and jurisdictions, they come together to challenge assumptions, exchange ideas, learn from one another, and turn imagination into action. The result is a dynamic global environment where relationships spark collaboration and a shared vision for the future begins to take shape.
